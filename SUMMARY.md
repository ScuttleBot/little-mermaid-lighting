## Key Findings

### 1. Water Caustic Effects (Most Important)
**Professional Technique (Disney 2023 film):**
- Suspended water trays above lights with stirring apparatus
- Creates organic, photorealistic caustic patterns

**High School Options:**
- **Easiest:** 2x Chauvet Abyss USB water effect lights ($400-500 each)
- **Most versatile:** Moving lights with dual gobo wheels + rotation
- **Budget:** 4x Source4 + TwinSpins + water ripple gobos

### 2. Color Palette by Location
**Different scenes need different color identities:**

- **Ariel's Grotto:** Light blues, turquoise (hopeful, curious)
- **Triton's Kingdom:** Jewel tones - blues, purples, teals (regal, vibrant)
- **Ursula's Lair:** Dark purples, deep blues (sinister, shadowy)
- **Above Water:** Warm whites, ambers, golden (realistic, sunlit)

### 3. Lighting Principles for Underwater
- **Backlight + toplight dominance** in blues/teals (water overhead feeling)
- **Warm front/side light** (ambers, light pinks) to pull actors out of blue
- **Common mistake:** Over-saturating with blue wash - "less is more"
- **Movement is key:** Slow gobo rotation, varying speeds between fixtures

### 4. Fog for Water/Land Transitions
**Technique:**
- Dense low fog at deck level = water surface
- Backlit for ethereal boundary
- Gradual lighting shift (cool â†’ warm) over 30-60 seconds
- Fog dissipates upward as character "rises"

**Critical:** Rehearse extensively - timing varies with humidity/HVAC

### 5. The "Sunken Theater" Concept
From ABC Little Mermaid Live! production designer:
- Entire environment feels submerged
- Organic curved shapes (no hard edges)
- Layered effects: projection + LED + practical + lighting
- Constant vertical movement creates "floating" feeling

## Minimum Budget Setup
**Starting from scratch: $800-1500**

Essential equipment:
- 2x water effect lights OR 4x Source4 + TwinSpins + gobos
- 1x low fog machine
- Blue/teal/purple gels
- Warm accent gels (CTOs, light pinks)

## Gobo Recommendations
